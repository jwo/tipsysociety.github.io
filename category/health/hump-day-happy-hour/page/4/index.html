Hump Day Happy Hour: Gluwein

December 22, 2010 by sarah

This German version of a spiced wine (pronounced “clue wine”) used to be a staple at my house during Christmastime. We haven’t made this wine for several years, but I think  this will be the year we bring it back…sounds delish and I wish I could partake!

  • 4 quarts of dry red wine (you can use a cheap wine for this)
  • 1 pint of brandy
  • 1 cup of sugar
  • 6 cinnamon sticks
  • 12 whole cloves
  • 1/8 tsp allspice
  • 1/8 tsp mace
  • 2 oranges, sliced
  • 1 lemon sliced

Pour the wine into a large pot and begin heating over low heat. As it begins to warm, add sugar and spices. Stir until sugar is dissolved.

Add the brandy.

Add the lemon and orange.  Heat thoroughly but do not allow the wine to ever boil.

Serve hot and garnish with orange slices.

Hump Day Happy Hour: Berry Christmas Punch

December 15, 2010 by court

I consider this the home stretch of planning all the get togethers and meals surrounding the holiday season.  This is sure to be a crowd pleaser, looks its takes a bit of prep, but could really pay off!

  • 1.5 liters Ocean Spray raspberry cranberry, well chilled
  • 2  bottles of sparkling wine, well chilled
  • 1/2 cup Cointreau
  • 2 limes, quartered
  • 1 cup strawberries, washed, halved
  • 3/4 cup blueberries
  • 1/2 cup raspberries
  • 1/4 cup fresh mint leaves

Pour the cranberry juice, sparkling wine and Cointreau into a punch bowl. Use your hands to squeeze some of the juice from the limes into the punch. Stir. Add the squeezed limes to the punch. Add the strawberries, blueberries, raspberries and mint leaves. Dunzo.

Some of the other tipsy gals have really cool pitchers to serve this out of, like the ones shown here, I think it would be a shoe-in to a berry merry Christmas!
